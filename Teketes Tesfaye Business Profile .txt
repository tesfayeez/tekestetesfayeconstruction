INTRODUCTION
Who we are

Over the years, Tekeste Tesfaye B.C & WWC has built a reputation for excellence and reliability in the construction industry, supported by its strong technical and financial standing.
Since its inception, Tekeste Tesfaye B.C & WWC has successfully completed a wide array of construction projects, ranging from residential buildings and educational facilities (including primary, secondary, and technical schools) to health centers, clinics, water supply systems, gravel roads, bridges, and irrigation projects. METHODOLOGY
We employ a well-established, systematic methodology tailored to deliver construction projects that meet the highest standards of quality, efficiency, and safety. Our approach integrates time-tested construction practices with modern technologies to optimize every phase of the project lifecycle, from planning through to completion.
1. Pre-Construction Phase
1.1. Planning and Preparation
The foundation of any successful construction project lies in thorough planning and preparation. We commence each project with a robust pre-construction phase that includes detailed planning, resource allocation, and site preparation.
* Site Assessment and Surveying with GIS Tools:
Utilizing Geographic Information Systems (GIS), we conduct comprehensive site assessments to collect and analyze spatial data. This technology allows us to precisely map out the terrain, understand topographical challenges, and identify potential environmental constraints. This early-stage data gathering ensures that all site-specific factors are considered, reducing the likelihood of unexpected issues during construction.
* Development of Work Plans and Scheduling Using Project Management Software:
We use project management software, such as Microsoft Project or Primavera P6, to create detailed work plans that outline the sequence of construction activities, allocate resources, and set project timelines. This software facilitates the development of Gantt charts and network diagrams, ensuring that all tasks are properly scheduled and that resource use is optimized throughout the project.
* Design Coordination and Visualization with BIM:
Building Information Modeling (BIM) is employed to create accurate, 3D digital models of the project. These models provide a visual representation of the design, allowing for early detection of any potential clashes between structural, mechanical, electrical, and plumbing systems. By resolving these issues in the planning phase, we minimize costly rework and ensure a smoother construction process.
* Mobilization of Equipment and Personnel:
We ensure that all necessary equipment and personnel are mobilized to the project site in a timely manner. This includes setting up temporary facilities such as site offices, storage areas, and worker accommodations, which are strategically planned using GIS data to optimize site layout and operations.
1.2. Pre-Construction Preparation Activities
Before commencing construction, a series of preparation activities are undertaken to ensure the site is ready for the work ahead:
   * Site Clearing and Grading:
The designated construction areas are cleared of any vegetation, debris, and other obstructions using traditional methods combined with GIS data to optimize clearing activities. Grading is carried out to create a level foundation for subsequent construction activities, using GPS-enabled machinery for precision.
   * Foundation and Earthwork Preparation:
We execute detailed excavation and foundation preparation based on BIM models that provide exact specifications. This includes excavating for foundations, trenches, and other substructure components to the precise dimensions and depths required. Our use of project management software ensures these tasks are sequenced efficiently, reducing downtime and overlaps.
   * Installation of Temporary Utilities and Facilities:
Temporary utilities such as water, electricity, and site access are set up to support construction activities. These facilities are established following a carefully crafted plan informed by GIS data, ensuring minimal disruption and efficient operation.
2. Construction Phase
2.1. Substructure Construction
During this phase, we focus on constructing the foundational elements of the project, ensuring structural integrity and longevity:
      * Excavation and Foundation Work:
Excavation for foundations and other below-ground structures is performed with precision, guided by BIM models to ensure accurate alignment and depth. Foundations are constructed using high-strength concrete, with reinforcement placement verified against BIM specifications to ensure structural integrity.
      * Masonry and Concrete Works:
Masonry and concrete works are carried out following the BIM-based design specifications, ensuring all walls, columns, and other structural elements are built to exact standards. We utilize mechanical mixers and modern techniques to achieve the right consistency and quality in concrete production, thereby enhancing the durability and strength of the substructure.
      * Waterproofing and Backfilling:
Substructures are protected from water ingress using advanced waterproofing membranes and materials. Backfilling is done layer by layer with selected materials, compacted using modern compaction techniques to achieve optimal density and stability as specified in the design.
2.2. Superstructure Construction
The superstructure phase involves building all above-ground elements, meticulously planned and executed to ensure quality and durability:
         * Structural Framing and Assembly:
The construction of beams, columns, and slabs is performed with careful attention to detail, ensuring that all structural components are aligned according to BIM models. This phase includes the use of prefabricated components where appropriate to reduce on-site labor and enhance construction speed.
         * Wall Construction and Finishing:
Walls are constructed using high-quality materials specified in the BIM model, ensuring consistency in both appearance and structural performance. Exterior and interior finishing works, such as plastering, painting, and installation of fixtures, are coordinated using project management software to ensure timely completion.
         * Roofing and Waterproofing:
Roofing structures are installed following BIM guidelines, using durable materials that provide effective protection against weather conditions. Waterproofing techniques are applied to ensure long-term resilience against water damage.
3. Post-Construction Phase
3.1. Quality Assurance and Handover
Once construction is completed, a thorough quality assurance process is conducted to ensure that all aspects of the project meet the highest standards:
            * Inspections and Testing with Digital Tools:
We perform comprehensive inspections and testing of all structural elements using advanced digital tools to ensure they meet safety and quality standards. The BIM model is referenced throughout the inspection process to verify that the construction aligns with the original design and specifications.
            * Digital Documentation and Final Handover:
We prepare and deliver digital documentation, including as-built BIM models, operation manuals, and maintenance schedules, to the client. This ensures that the client has a complete understanding of the project and the necessary information for ongoing maintenance.
3.2. Site Cleanup and Demobilization
               * Environmental Restoration and Site Cleanup:
The site is restored to its original condition or as per the client's requirements, using sustainable practices such as recycling construction waste and restoring natural vegetation.
               * Safe Demobilization of Equipment and Personnel:
Equipment and personnel are safely demobilized from the site following strict protocols to ensure safety and regulatory compliance. All necessary documentation is completed and submitted to local authorities as part of the project closeout process.
4. Commitment to Sustainability and Safety
We are committed to sustainability and safety throughout all project phases:
                  * Sustainable Practices with GIS Support:
We employ GIS to monitor environmental impacts and ensure sustainable practices are followed. This includes efficient site management and minimal disruption to the surrounding environment.
                  * Health and Safety Management:
Strict health and safety standards are maintained on all sites, with regular training, audits, and monitoring to ensure the safety of all workers and stakeholders.
Conclusion
Our methodology combines traditional construction practices with modern technology to ensure efficient, high-quality, and sustainable project delivery. Our use of GIS, BIM, and Project Management Software enhances our ability to plan, execute, and manage projects effectively, providing our clients with superior construction solutions.
















































PROPOSED METHOD OF WORKING AND SCHEDULE
We adopt a systematic and efficient approach to construction projects, combining traditional craftsmanship with modern technology to ensure timely and successful project completion. Our proposed method of working is structured around several key phases, from pre-mobilization to final handover, ensuring that each stage is meticulously planned, executed, and monitored for quality and compliance.
1. Pre-Mobilization Phase
The pre-mobilization phase involves all preparatory activities necessary to set the foundation for the project:
                     * Pre-Construction Planning:
We begin with comprehensive project planning, which includes risk assessments, resource allocation, and the development of a project strategy using Project Management Software. Early stakeholder engagement ensures alignment of objectives and expectations.
                     * Site Analysis and Setup:
Utilizing Geographic Information Systems (GIS), we conduct a thorough site analysis to assess conditions, topography, and environmental factors. This data informs the strategic setup of temporary facilities such as access roads, storage areas, site offices, and utility connections, ensuring smooth operational flow.
                     * Equipment and Material Mobilization:
All necessary equipment and materials are mobilized to the site in accordance with the project plan. This includes ensuring that heavy machinery, construction materials, and safety equipment are available and ready for use.
                     * Regulatory Compliance and Permitting:
We obtain all required permits and approvals from local authorities, ensuring compliance with all regulatory requirements. Digital tools are used to streamline documentation and communication with regulatory bodies.
2. Mobilization Phase
During the mobilization phase, we bring all resources, equipment, and personnel to the construction site:
                        * Deployment of Personnel and Equipment:
Key personnel, including project managers, site engineers, and construction crews, are deployed to the site. Heavy equipment such as excavators, bulldozers, and cranes are strategically positioned to ensure optimal access and use.
                        * Establishment of Contractor’s Camp and Site Facilities:
We establish the contractor’s camp, which includes living quarters, dining areas, and sanitation facilities for the workforce. Site facilities are equipped with the necessary utilities such as water, electricity, and communication systems.
                        * Site Preparation and Clearing:
We begin site preparation activities, which include clearing vegetation, debris, and other obstacles. Site grading and leveling are performed using GPS-enabled machinery to achieve the required elevation and slope.
3. Construction and Operation Phase
The construction and operation phase encompasses all activities related to the actual construction of the project:
A. Substructure Works
                           * Foundation Excavation and Earthworks:
Foundation excavation and earthworks are executed according to the specifications outlined in the Building Information Modeling (BIM) design. Precise excavation techniques are employed to ensure that foundations are correctly aligned and at the required depth.
                           * Formwork and Reinforcement Installation:
Formwork and reinforcement bars for foundations, beams, and columns are installed according to the BIM model specifications. All materials are thoroughly inspected for quality and compliance with engineering standards.
                           * Concrete Pouring and Curing:
Concrete is poured into the formwork using high-efficiency mixers to ensure the correct mix ratio and consistency. Curing is performed following standard practices to achieve optimal strength and durability.
B. Superstructure Works
                              * Erection of Structural Components:
Structural components, including columns, beams, and slabs, are assembled with precision guided by the BIM model to ensure proper alignment and fit. Prefabricated elements may be used to enhance construction speed and quality.
                              * Wall and Roof Construction:
Walls are constructed, and roofing materials are installed following the design specifications. Advanced waterproofing techniques are applied to protect the building envelope from weather elements.
                              * Mechanical, Electrical, and Plumbing (MEP) Installations:
MEP installations are coordinated to ensure seamless integration of all systems. BIM is used to detect and resolve potential clashes between systems before installation begins.
C. Finishing Works
                                 * Interior and Exterior Finishes:
Finishing works, including plastering, painting, flooring, and ceiling installations, are performed according to client specifications. High-quality materials and skilled craftsmanship are employed to ensure a professional finish.
                                 * Installation of Fixtures and Fittings:
All fixtures and fittings, such as doors, windows, sanitary fixtures, and electrical components, are installed in accordance with the design plan. Final adjustments and calibrations are made to ensure proper functionality.
4. Post-Construction Phase
The post-construction phase is focused on quality assurance, project closeout, and client handover:
                                    * Quality Control and Testing:
We conduct comprehensive quality control and testing of all structural elements and systems to ensure they meet or exceed industry standards. This includes load testing, water tightness testing, and material quality checks.
                                    * Site Cleanup and Restoration:
The construction site is cleaned up by removing temporary facilities, waste materials, and equipment. The site is restored to its original condition or as per the client’s requirements, ensuring minimal environmental impact.
                                    * Final Handover and Documentation:
All necessary documentation, including as-built drawings, operation manuals, and maintenance schedules, is prepared and provided to the client. A final walkthrough is conducted with the client to address any concerns and ensure satisfaction with the completed project.
5. Schedule Overview
The duration of each project phase can vary significantly based on factors such as project size, scope, complexity, and site-specific conditions. A typical project may include the following phases:
                                       * Pre-Mobilization Activities:
                                       * Planning, permitting, and initial site setup.
                                       * Mobilization Activities:
                                       * Deployment of personnel and equipment, establishment of site facilities.
                                       * Substructure Construction:
                                       * Excavation, foundation work, and concrete pouring.
                                       * Superstructure Construction:
                                       * Erection of structural components, wall construction, and MEP installations.
                                       * Finishing Works:
                                       * Interior and exterior finishes, installation of fixtures and fittings.
                                       * Post-Construction Activities:
                                       * Quality control, site cleanup, and final handover.
Please note that the actual timeline for each phase is subject to change based on specific project requirements and unforeseen site conditions.
Conclusion
By following this structured methodology and adapting our schedule to the specific needs of each project, we ensure that all projects are delivered on time, within budget, and to the highest standards of quality. Our approach leverages modern tools such as GIS, BIM, and Project Management Software to enhance project planning, execution, and monitoring, ensuring successful outcomes for our clients.
